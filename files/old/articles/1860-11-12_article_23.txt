#TEXT: ;;; Pardoned.;;; --George W. Young, of Augusta, Me, who was convicted in  June, 1858, of advising and assisting G. W. L. Burnham in robbing the U. S. mail, between Augusta and Belfast, and was sentenced to fifteen years imprisonment in the State Prison, has been pardoned by the President of the United States.--Young has been in the State Prison two years and five months.;;;
